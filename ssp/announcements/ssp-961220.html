American Poetry online

Last week we received the American Poetry Database. Because of similarities with other Chadwyck-Healey collections, the development time has been fairly short. Beginning next week, we will have a beta version available at:

http://www.hti.umich.edu/english/ampo/

For those institutions accessing collections on www.hti.umich.edu, please feel free to circulate the URL and begin testing. Your feedback is appreciated. A production version should be available by mid-January.

For institutions accessing the collection on a server at their home institution, we will begin distribution of the built indexes, interface, and middleware in late January. Again, for institutions mounting locally, please make sure that we have a letter on file from Chadwyck-Healey confirming your license if you wish to mount the collection locally. To date, we have letters for Indiana/IUPUI, and Rice.

New server
The new server has arrived and we have completed installation and testing. Beginning tomorrow, the address www.hti.umich.edu will migrate from the current machine to the new server. Performance should improve significantly, both on the server and with regard to bandwidth. The new machine has several times the capacity/performance of the current machine, and is attached directly to the campus fiber (FDDI) backbone. Because it also uses a different operating system, we may see some peculiarities in IP-name resolution, and consequently some new issues with access restrictions. Please bear with us as we resolve these. Working through your systems/technical representative will help us to better track valid numeric addresses and resolve problems quickly.

Contacts list

We have posted a list of campus contacts on the SGML Server Program web space:

http://www.hti.umich.edu/misc/ssp/contacts.html

Please review the list and contact me with corrections and additions. I hope you will encourage staff to work through the contacts in reporting problems and raising questions. It will help us in a number of ways, not least of which reducing the number of times we answer a question. In particular, it will be helpful in resolving problems of access restrictions. To help you comply with the terms of the Chadwyck-Healey and OUP licenses (i.e., by restricting access to valid subnets), we need to be sure that the person reporting a purportedly valid IP address is providing accurate information. It will also help the Technical/Systems contact person to build and keep a complete listing for future work.

Summer workshop

As part of the SGML Server Program, you are all invited to come to Ann Arbor for training in several aspects of support for the text resources. We have begun planning for two summer workshops, to be offered in conjunction with the School of Information. Tentative plans are for the first to be workshop to be held from May 27th-31st, with the SSP-specific portion to be offered on the final two days (Friday and Saturday). SGML Server Program participants are invited to send two persons to the SSP portion of the workshop at no charge for the workshop. The first three days will cover SGML encoding (use of Author/Editor), scanning/OCR, and several related topics. The fee for the week-long workshop will be set later. We have been able to negotiate a 50% rate for SSP participants for the week-long workshop, so that it will be possible to attend the final two days at no charge, or the entire week at a lesser rate. More information will follow as we firm up details.
