SSP Text Encoding and User Support Workshop


Location: Faculty Exploratory, second floor Hatcher Graduate Library
Dates: July 28-31, 1999
Instructors: Chris Powell, University of Michigan
The instructor will be supported by University of Michigan programming staff and interface specialist.

Course objectives

  1. Become familiar with the basics of text encoding using SGML
  2. Introduce principles of document analysis, including DTD reading and using PAT
  3. Discuss issues of interface design and build a rudimentary interface to a sample collection
  4. Gain a basic understanding of the process of deploying an electronic collection for Web access
  5. Discuss issues of user support for electronic collections and of the relationship of the digital library to the traditional library

Required readings:

  • SGML Primer
  • The Gentle Introduction to SGML from the TEI Guidelines
  • Introductory portions of the Pat 5.0 manual

Text collections and other projects to think about (a biased sample):

  • DLPS Homepage: gate way to all our collections plus lots of stuff on how we think about things around here
  • HTI Collections
  • The Victorian Women Writers Project
  • Making of America
  • HTI Staff guidelines
  • SGML Creation and Delivery: The Humanities Text Initiative

Other useful things:

  • TEI Listserv -- send email to TEI-L@LISTSERV.UIC.EDU with
          subscribe Your Name
    in the body of the message.
  • Build your own TEI DTD -- not TEILite, which is already built, but a combination of other core sets.
  • ISO 639-2 Language Codes -- we use the three letter codes, because we need to distinguish between English, Middle English, and Old English.

Please bring texts you are questions you have, texts you are working on, and anything else you would like to share with others or ask others about.
