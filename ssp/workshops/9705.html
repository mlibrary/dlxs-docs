SGML Server Program

SGML Encoding and System Implementation

For more information about enrolling, please see the School of Information Digital Tool Kit web page.

Background

This advanced course covers elements of SGML text creation, from scanning and OCR through online implementation. The curriculum is designed particularly for staff from institutions subscribing the SGML Server Program (SSP) from the University of Michigan Humanities Text Initiative, but is also relevant for persons using Author/Editor, Open Text's search engine, and those generally interested in putting SGML on the Web. The focus of the one week session will be on those collections offered through the SGML Server Program ( http://www.hti.umich.edu/misc/ssp/), and will include a focus on using the TEI Guidelines and the Encoded Archival Description.

 

Prerequisites

Readings

  1. Gentle Introduction to SGML
  2. SGML Primer
  3. Markup Systems and the Future of Scholarly Text Processing (published several times, including CACM 30 (November 1987):933-47 and in Digital Word, ed. Landau and Delaney)
  4. Elements Available in All TEI Documents (a.k.a. "core elements"), and Default Text Structure (from TEI Guidelines)
  5. EAD Application Guidelines

Software
Although not required, participants are encouraged to purchase and begin using SoftQuad’s Author/Editor and Panorama Pro before the workshop. Additionally, the advanced online mechanisms discussed in the workshop rely on OpenText’s search engine (version 5.x will be used).

Facilities
Most sessions will be held in the University of Michigan’s new Faculty Exploratory on the second floor of the Harlan Hatcher Graduate Library. The Exploratory is well-equipped with high-end personal computers, robust networking, and large-format displays, and is conveniently located one floor from the HTI’s production offices. Occasional sessions will be held in the HTI’s production areas (302 and 309 Hatcher) to explore the use of high-speed scanning, and batch OCR.
