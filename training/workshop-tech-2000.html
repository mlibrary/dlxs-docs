DLXS Programmers & Technical Workshop

Location: Faculty Exploratory, second floor Hatcher Graduate Library
Dates: August 8-12, 2000
Instructors: Alan Pagliere and Phil Farber, University of Michigan

Course objectives

  1. Become familiar with the DLXS Text & Image Class middleware
Prerequisites:
  • some familiarity with SGML.
  • familiarity with perl5.
  • have seen the kinds of online collections DLPS makes available already (see http://www.umdl.umich.edu/ and http://www.hti.umich.edu/ for examples).
  • have either the PAT50 package from OpenText or XPAT package from DLXS.
  • Note: though attendees will likely have some SGML that they are interested in making an online interface for, the TextClass system presumes, and therefore this workshop presumes, that the text has been encoded in line with the concepts presented in the DLXS Public Service & Text Encoders Workshop.
    Time permitting, classes other than Image Class and the new object-oriented Text Class will be discussed.

    • Middle English Compendium, including various and diverf collections: Corpus of Middle English Prose and Verse, the Middle English Dictionary, and the Hyperbibliography of Middle English.
    • Physicians' GenRx

      • Other miscellaneous collections (with interesting features and functionality)

    • Pictures of Record (Text and images data in separate collections on separate machines, pat50 server daemon)
    Tentative Schedule:
    1. Pat 5.0/XPAT overview (indexing, queries, etc)
    2. Image Processing (Image Class)
    3. Configuring the middleware: (Image & Text Class)
      • Putting middleware online in client's environment
      • Configuring middleware, SGML, DTD for client's collection
      • Programming Pat 5.0/XPAT queries in Perl
      • Sub-classing the Text Class for searching and display of results
